Digital Care Companion – Why Yours Isn't Working (And What Actually Does)

By Daniel R Hungerford, Founder • January 15, 2025

You bought the device. You set it up with hope in your heart and desperation in your gut. Maybe it was ElliQ, maybe it was another "revolutionary" digital care companion that promised to solve your parent's loneliness and your crushing guilt.

But here's what happened next: nothing. Or worse, something that made everything harder.

Your mom still calls you six times a day. The device sits there, blinking uselessly, while she stares at it with the same confused expression she had when you first showed it to her. And you're left wondering: "What did I do wrong?"

The Problem with Most Digital Care Companions

After years of building AI systems and watching families struggle with existing solutions, I've identified the core issues that make most digital care companions fail:

1. They're Built for Tech People, Not Real People

Most digital companions are designed by engineers who've never sat with an 85-year-old trying to figure out how to turn on a tablet. They assume users will read manuals, remember passwords, and navigate complex interfaces. But your parent isn't a tech-savvy millennial—they're someone who just wants to feel connected.

2. They Don't Understand Context

Generic responses and scripted conversations feel hollow. When your mom says "I'm scared," she needs more than "I understand you're feeling scared." She needs someone who remembers that her husband died last year, that she's worried about falling, and that her daughter lives 200 miles away.

3. They're Reactive, Not Proactive

Most devices wait for your parent to initiate conversation. But loneliness doesn't work on a schedule. Your mom needs someone who checks in on her, remembers to ask about her doctor's appointment, and notices when she hasn't spoken to anyone in days.

The Reality: 43% of adults over 65 report feeling lonely, yet 70% of digital health tools are abandoned within the first month. The problem isn't the technology—it's how it's designed.
